Biography

I am on the job market, looking for full-time research positions in industry.

Hey there! I am a fifth-year PhD student at National University of Singapore, co-supervised by Prof. Li-Shiuan Peh and Prof. Tulika Mitra. During my PhD, I have had the opportunity to intern at AMD Research and Renesas, and I am an incoming research intern at IBM Research, Yorktown Heights.

Prior to this, I completed my undergraduate degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering at IIIT Delhi, where I worked in the AMS Lab. I also spent time at HESL Lab at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. After graduation, I worked for two years at Qualcomm India as a CPU RTL Design Engineer.

My research lies at the intersection of VLSI design, computer architecture, and AI. I develop hardware–software co-design solutions to address performance and efficiency bottlenecks in the post-Dennard era. Specifically, my work focuses on reconfigurable architectures and GPU-based accelerators for irregular and mixed-precision workloads driven by sparsity and quantization—key enablers of AI model compression. I design LLVM-based compiler toolchains to map compressed models efficiently onto custom accelerators and validate these designs through real-world chip tapeouts. By advancing performance, energy efficiency, and reliability, my work aims to lower the carbon footprint of modern computing systems.

In the past, I have been fortunate to collaborate with Prof. Sujay Deb [IIIT-Delhi], Prof. Lam Siew Kei [NTU], and Saru Vig [A*STAR].
